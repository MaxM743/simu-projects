Heat equation

Formal definition

The heat equation on a plan $\mathcal{S}$ is defined as :
$$\dfrac{\partial T}{\partial t}=D\left[\dfrac{\partial^2T}{\partial x^2}+\dfrac{\partial^2T}{\partial y^2}\right]+Q(x, y)$$
where $D$ is a constant depending on the wall properties and $g$ a function representing a power source applied in the wall.

Finite Difference

Considering the wall $\mathcal{S}$ of width $L$ and height $H$ (see. previous figure) we can discretize the domain in $N_x\times N_y$ points such as $\Delta x=\dfrac{L}{N_x}$ and $\Delta y=\dfrac{H}{N_y}$. The heat equation for each point inside the domain we can write :
$$T_{i,j}^{n+1}=T_{i, j}^n + \alpha \left[T_{i, j-1}^n+T_{i, j+1}^n-2T_{i, j}^n\right]+\beta \left[T_{i-1, j}^n+T_{i+1, j}^n-2T_{i, j}^n\right]+\Delta t Q_{i, j}^{n}$$
where :
$$\alpha=D\dfrac{\Delta t}{\Delta x^2} \text{ et } \beta=D\dfrac{\Delta t}{\Delta y^2}$$
